Welcome to the LACE Evidence Hub. You can use this site to find or share research evidence relating to learning analytics.

Learning analytics is the measurement, collection, analysis and reporting of data about learners and their contexts, in order to understand and optimise learning and the environments in which it occurs.

On this site, you can search for, or share, evidence related to these four propositions:

  • Learning analytics improve learning outcomes.
  • Learning analytics improve learning support and teaching, including retention, completion and progression.
  • Learning analytics are taken up and used widely, including deployment at scale.
  • Learning analytics are used in an ethical way.

Use the search options on the right to find evidence for and against these claims, as well as links to projects that are exploring these issues.

You can filter your search in order to look for evidence related to a specific country or educational sector. You can also explore key words and tags, or focus just on positive, negative or neutral evidence.

The Evidence tab above provides different ways of visualizing the evidence in the Hub.

If you know of any evidence that isn't already included in the Hub, please select the 'Contribute' tab to add it.

To find out more about the Learning Analytics Community Exchange (LACE) project, visit our website, or subscribe to our newsletter.

The LACE Evidence Hub is run by the Institute of Educational Technology at The Open University, UK, as part of the LACE project, using software developed for the OER Research Hub at the Institute of Educational Technology. It is based on WordPress, and the code is open source, freely available on GitHub. For more information about the LACE project, see the main LACE project website.
